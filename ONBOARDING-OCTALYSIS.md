# The First Session — a 20-interaction onboarding tutorial (Octalysis)

> *Companion to [INFINITE-GAME.md](./INFINITE-GAME.md). If the operator is a game, the founder is the
> **player**, and every good game opens with a **tutorial** that teaches the loop while it hooks. This is
> that tutorial: the **first 20 interactions**, each engineered against Yu-kai Chou's **Octalysis** 8 core
> drives, each teaching one piece of the **micro/meso/macro** loop, each revealing one operator capability.*

---

## 0. The design doctrine (why the hat-balance is the whole game)

Octalysis sorts motivation into **8 Core Drives** on an octagon:

| # | Core Drive | Hat | Brain | In the operator |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Epic Meaning & Calling** | White (apex) | **mid brain** | You're not building a product — you're starting an *infinite game* with a Just Cause. |
| 2 | **Development & Accomplishment** | White | Left | Visible progress: the mint completes, artifacts ship, the viability gauge fills. |
| 3 | **Empowerment of Creativity & Feedback** | White | Right | You shape the brand and the operator *re-projects on-brand instantly* — meaningful choice + feedback. |
| 4 | **Ownership & Possession** | — | Left | The vision, the brand-DNA, the world-state are **yours** — the Just Cause is your anchor. |
| 5 | **Social Influence & Relatedness** | — | Right | You *meet* your customer (ICP-NPC) and your mirror (Founder-NPC) as living characters. |
| 6 | **Scarcity & Impatience** | Black | Left | Runway is finite — **solvency is a real bound**, not a fake timer. Moves are gated. |
| 7 | **Unpredictability & Curiosity** | Black | Right | The ICP surfaces *surprising* pains; the market co-evolves (Red Queen). What will it reveal next? |
| 8 | **Loss & Avoidance** | Black (apex) | **mid brain** | The fear that matters: **dropping out of the game** (leaving the viability kernel) — drift is the enemy. |

**The doctrine for an infinite game:** lead **White Hat** (1·2·3) — intrinsic, empowering, *sustainable* —
because black-hat urgency (6·7·8) creates engagement that **burns out**, and burning the founder out *ends the
infinite game*. We use black hat **only grounded in something real**: scarcity = the *actual* solvency bound;
unpredictability = the *actual* moving market; loss = *actual* drift out of viability. **No fake countdowns. No
dark patterns.** And we **open and close White Hat** — the first move and the last move are Epic Meaning +
Ownership, so the player's lasting feeling is *calling and possession*, not anxiety.

### The vertical axis is the **mid-brain** — and it activates **noesis**

The octagon has **three** brain regions, not two. Left-brain (`2·4·6`) is extrinsic / logic;
right-brain (`3·5·7`) is intrinsic / emotion; and the **vertical axis — `1` Epic Meaning at the top,
`8` Loss & Avoidance at the bottom — is the *mid-brain*: the deepest motivational axis, *meaning ↔
survival*.** These two drives are neither analytical nor merely creative — they are **existential**: the
calling that pulls you in, and the dread of dropping out of the game.

**The design rule: when the mid-brain activates, activate `noesis`.** A mid-brain event — a moment of
genuine *Epic Meaning*, or a moment of *Loss / drift* — is **not** routed to a routine micro/meso/macro
tick. It is escalated to **noesis** — the operator's higher meaning-making / intuition layer (νόησις, the
*nous*; the same layer the DA speaks from). At the existential extremes the operator must be at its most
present: noesis reflects the *why*, speaks in the founder's deepest register, and — when the stakes are
real — hands the moment to the human. Mechanically, **`mid_brain(event) → invoke noesis`** sits beside the
Venn router: the router handles left/right-brain drives at their tick-rates; the **mid-brain bypass** routes
drives `1` and `8` straight to noesis, because those are the moments that decide whether the founder *stays
in the game*. The tutorial's noesis peaks: **#1 (The Calling), #13 · #16 · #17 · #18 (objection · gated
goal-move · the viability board · drift), and #20 (the game continues)** — the moments that must land as
*meaning*, not mechanics.

## 2. The 20 interactions

> `Layer` = which part of the loop it teaches (micro/meso/macro). `Reveals` = the operator capability unlocked.

### Phase A — Discovery / The Calling

| # | Interaction | What the founder sees / does | Core drive(s) | Layer | Reveals |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Calling** | A spare opening line: *"You're not building a product. You're starting an infinite game."* One button: *Plant a seed idea.* | **1 Epic Meaning** | macro | the infinite-game frame |
| 2 | **Name the Just Cause** | Founder writes their vision in one sentence; the operator pins it as the *near-invariant anchor* and shows it will barely move. | **1 Epic Meaning** + **4 Ownership** | macro | the vision anchor |
| 3 | **Plant the seed** | Founder drops a raw idea (a sentence / brand-config). The operator accepts the *seed idea* and says "watch." | **7 Curiosity** + 1 | macro→ | idea ingestion |

### Phase B — First Mint / Accomplishment

| # | Interaction | What the founder sees / does | Core drive(s) | Layer | Reveals |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | **Germination** | Genesis (meristem) runs; the founder watches the 7 waves complete live — a real progress bar, not a spinner. | **2 Accomplishment** + 7 | micro *(finite run)* | the genesis organ |
| 5 | **First light** | The minted brand-DNA appears — name, positioning, palette, voice — all at once. The first real win. | **2 Accomplishment** + **4 Ownership** | micro | the brand-spec output |
| 6 | **Claim it** | Founder accepts the brand-DNA (or edits one field). The world-state is now *theirs* and *mutable*. | **4 Ownership** + **3 Creativity** | micro | world-state mutability |
| 7 | **The on-brand meter** | A live gauge appears — the on-brand score `V = d(x, x*)`. Green = on-brand. First quantified feedback. | **2 Accomplishment** + **3 Feedback** | micro | the cortex / taste · the V meter |
| 8 | **First booster** | Founder nudges the voice tone; the operator re-projects *every* artifact on-brand **instantly**. Meaningful choice → immediate visible effect. | **3 Empowerment & Feedback** | meso | allostatic micro-correction |

### Phase C — Meet the Players

| # | Interaction | What the founder sees / does | Core drive(s) | Layer | Reveals |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 9 | **Meet your customer** | The ICP-NPC ("Mira") is introduced as a *living* character the founder can talk to. | **5 Social/Relatedness** + 7 | meso | the ICP-NPC |
| 10 | **Interrogate the ICP** | Founder asks Mira *"what actually hurts?"* — she answers; **pain-point vectors** light up in the brand space. | **7 Unpredictability** + **5 Social** | meso | pain-point vectors |
| 11 | **The resonance arrow** | The operator draws the *direction* to move positioning so Mira's pain resolves — a literal arrow the founder can follow. | **3 Creativity** + **2 Accomplishment** | meso→macro | resonance direction `g` |
| 12 | **Meet your mirror** | The Founder-NPC is introduced — it reflects the founder's own operating logic/taste; the *intent oracle*. | **4 Ownership** + **5 Social** | meso | the Founder-NPC |
| 13 | **A surprising objection** | Mira raises an objection the founder *didn't* expect — curiosity plus a small, productive "uh-oh." | **7 Unpredictability** + **8 Loss** | meso | co-evolution / objection signals |

### Phase D — The Loop (micro → meso → macro + the gates)

| # | Interaction | What the founder sees / does | Core drive(s) | Layer | Reveals |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14 | **Your first micro move** | Founder makes a tiny fine-tune (a CTA word); auto-applied, instantly reversible. *This is MICRO.* | **2 Accomplishment** + **3 Empowerment** | **micro** | the micro tick |
| 15 | **Error or intent?** | Founder redirects something; the operator asks the one-bit question — *bad step (reroll) or new intent (move the goal)?* *This is MESO.* | **3 Feedback** + **7 Curiosity** | **meso** | the error-vs-intent why-handler |
| 16 | **Move the goal (gated)** | Founder proposes a reposition; the **gate** asks for *real evidence* and shows the **trust-region** cap. *This is MACRO.* | **4 Ownership** + **8 Loss-Avoidance** | **macro** | setpoint move + the fail-closed gate |
| 17 | **The viability board** | The operator reveals the **viability margins** — solvency, mission-coherence. Runway is finite and *visible*. Real scarcity. | **6 Scarcity** + **8 Loss** | macro | the viability monitor + solvency bound |

### Phase E — The Infinite Hook

| # | Interaction | What the founder sees / does | Core drive(s) | Layer | Reveals |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 18 | **Don't drop out** | A drift is detected (a `V` spike). The operator warns: *"this moves you toward leaving the game."* Founder corrects; relief. | **8 Loss & Avoidance** + **2 Accomplishment** | meso | drift detection (Lyapunov) |
| 19 | **The replay** | Founder scrubs the replay log — the *whole life of the venture so far*, every move, learning accumulating. Deep ownership. | **4 Ownership** + **2 Accomplishment** | cross-run | the replay log / cross-run learning |
| 20 | **The game continues** | The operator goes dormant: *"the game never ends — I'll wake on your next move."* The founder is now **in** the infinite game — Just Cause, brand, customer, margin, loop, all live. | **1 Epic Meaning** + **4 Ownership** + **6 Anticipation** | macro | the wake loop / the infinite game |

## 3. Coverage & balance check

| Core drive | Hit at interactions | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Epic Meaning | 1, 2, 3, 20 | **bookends** (open + close White Hat ✅) |
| 2 Accomplishment | 4, 5, 7, 11, 14, 18, 19 | heavy (White Hat spine) |
| 3 Creativity & Feedback | 6, 7, 8, 11, 14, 15 | heavy (White Hat spine) |
| 4 Ownership | 2, 5, 6, 12, 16, 19, 20 | heavy (sustains) |
| 5 Social | 9, 10, 12 | moderate (the NPCs) |
| 6 Scarcity | 17, 20 | light & **grounded** (solvency, anticipation) |
| 7 Unpredictability | 3, 4, 9, 10, 13, 15 | moderate (curiosity engine) |
| 8 Loss & Avoidance | 13, 16, 17, 18 | light & **grounded** (real drift / real bound) |

**Verdict:** White-Hat-dominant (1·2·3·4 carry the spine), Black Hat present but **late and grounded** (6·7·8
cluster in Phase D–E and every instance maps to a *real* stake), and the arc **opens and closes White Hat**
(Epic Meaning + Ownership) — the correct shape for an *infinite* game, where the goal is a player who keeps
playing for years, not a dopamine spike that flames out.

## 4. Anti-pattern guardrails (so the tutorial doesn't become a casino)

- **No fake scarcity.** Every countdown is the *real* solvency runway; every "limited" is a *real* viability bound.
- **No manufactured loss.** Interaction 18's "don't drop out" fires only on a *real* `V`-spike, never on a script.
- **No engagement for engagement's sake.** Each interaction teaches a real capability and advances the venture;
  delete any step that only manipulates.
- **The last feeling is Calling, not anxiety.** Interaction 20 is deliberately White Hat — the player leaves
  feeling *ownership of an infinite game*, not fear of a timer.
