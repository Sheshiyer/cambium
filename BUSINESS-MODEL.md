# Business model — free left brain, paid right brain

## The thesis

The system has two halves, and the **free/paid line falls exactly on the seam between them**:

| | Left brain — **the build** | Right brain — **the taste** |
|---|---|---|
| What | the 40 skill clusters (develop · maintain · market · ops), the conductor, **brandmint genesis** (one-shot: idea → brand kit) | the **aesthetic conscience** — on-brand QA on *every* artifact + the **design-memory / noesis cortex** that *learns your brand's taste over time* |
| Nature | **deterministic, commoditising** — anyone can build tools; LLMs make them cheaper monthly | **learned, personal, compounding** — it gets better at *your* taste the more you use it |
| Price | **free** (open-core) | **subscription** |
| Why | adoption flywheel: free genesis + free build → mass usage, viral, top-of-funnel | the moat: recurring, defensible, value compounds |

**One line:** *we give you the hands for free; you pay for the conscience.*

## Why this is the right split (not arbitrary)

1. **The build is a race to zero; taste is not.** Code-gen, scaffolding, and one-shot brand kits are commoditising fast — giving them away buys adoption you couldn't charge much for anyway. **Taste** — staying on-brand across a thousand artifacts, and *learning* what "on-brand" means for *you* — is the part that doesn't commoditise, because it's **personal and accumulating.**
2. **The moat is the memory, not the model.** Anyone can call the same image/text models. What competitors can't copy is **your brand's learned aesthetic DNA** — the design-memory + noesis cortex accumulating every on-brand decision you've ever made. That's a proprietary, compounding asset. *Switch away → you lose the learned taste.*
3. **It's recurring by nature.** A brand is minted once (free), but it needs to stay on-brand **forever** — every new page, ad, email, product render. On-brand QA is a per-artifact, every-day need → a subscription, not a one-time sale.
4. **Data network effect, single-tenant.** Unusually, the flywheel runs **per customer**: the more *you* use it, the better it knows *you*. Retention + expansion are built into the product mechanics, not bolted on.

## Packaging

| Tier | Who | What you get | Shape |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Genesis** *(free / open-core)* | founders, devs, indie hackers | mint **1 brand** (the full brand system), the build clusters, the conductor, the default render backend | self-serve, OSS-friendly — the funnel |
| **Taste** *(paid, per brand / mo)* | anyone shipping continuously | **ongoing on-brand QA** on every artifact · the **learning cortex** (your brand's taste, remembered + sharpening) · premium render (Nano Banana) · reference-anchored campaigns · semantic visual-QA | the core SaaS — the moat |
| **Portfolio** *(paid, multi-tenant)* | agencies · studios · holdcos | **snow-gloves-os** — run a *portfolio* of brands, each with its own learned taste; the full autonomous operator (genesis → build → market → operate → learn) per tenant | the platform / enterprise lift |

## Positioning lines
- *"Genesis is free. Taste is the subscription."*
- *"The build is a commodity. The taste is the moat — and it's yours, learned, forever."*
- *"Mint a brand for free. Pay to keep it on-brand — and to make the system know it better than you do."*

## The land-and-expand loop
**Free genesis** (mint a brand) → **paid Taste** (keep it on-brand; the cortex starts learning) → **Portfolio** (a studio runs many brands, each remembered) → every cycle the cortex deepens → **retention + expansion compound.**

## Analogues (open-core where the *intelligence* is paid)
- Supabase / GitLab — OSS core, paid scale/features. Cambium's twist: the paid layer isn't "more features," it's **accumulated, personal intelligence** that *can't* be forked.
- The right mental model isn't "freemium features" — it's **"the model is free; the memory is paid."**
