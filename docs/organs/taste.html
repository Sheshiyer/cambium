🎨 taste cortex — set the taste

the aesthetic conscience — the paid right brain. It holds the brand's taste, projects every artifact toward what is on-brand, and refuses what drifts.

Sheshiyer/skill-clusters · taste/ taste / the conscience brand-dna → taste-brief 💲 gated (NIM)

1Overview

The taste cortex takes a brand's brand-DNA and emits the on-brand taste-brief: it resolves any request into the feel, the reference cluster, and the constraints that make an artifact read as this brand. It then scores any artifact on-brand (kit-qa), rerolls until it is on-brand, and — over time — learns the brand's taste from which results were used versus rejected. It is the right brain to brandmint's left: brandmint decides what, the taste cortex decides whether it feels right.

2Internal architecture — the self-similar Φ

A conductor hub fans into capability spokes; two amber spokes verify and heal; the paid NIM cortex supplies the embeddings; pluggable backends render.

4Data flow + contract

brand-dna → taste-resolve (embed request via NIM → nearest on-brand refs) → taste-brief (feel + cluster) any artifact → kit-qa → pass | reroll

The contract across the seam is brand-dna → taste-brief. taste-resolve embeds the request in the paid NIM cosine space, recalls the nearest on-brand references, and returns a brief carrying the feel and the reference cluster. Any produced artifact is then routed through kit-qa: it either passes the on-brand gate or is sent back to reroll.

5Homeostasis role

The taste cortex is the contraction operator of the organism (Lᵢ<1). "Make it feel on-brand" is re-projection of an artifact toward the brand fixpoint x* — each pass of Φ_taste moves a candidate closer to on-brand, so the map is a contraction and the loop converges. kit-qa is the Lyapunov V check: it measures distance-from-on-brand and refuses any step that does not lower V. reroll is the self-heal loop: when a step would not lower V, it regenerates rather than accept drift. And the paid NIM is the moat — the gated embedding space is what makes "on-brand" measurable at all, so it cannot be trivially cloned.
